Iterative Design

  • Redesigning a Stagnant Website: Focused on creating a modern and user-friendly interface.
  • Branding Project with Figma: Developed a client website, starting with simplistic design prototypes and later adopting a creative new direction.
  • Portfolio Development: Explored various design ideas and settled on a design inspired by my country’s history and ancestors.

Redesigning a stagnant website

I analyzed competitors' websites to inform design decisions and aligned the new layout with industry standards. I chose a sailing company from my home country with an outdated website and crafted a new design emphasizing clarity and cleanliness. To reflect the company's sailing activities, I used relevant pictures and created a wavy design for sections with more text. Important elements like the tours section were displayed with clear call-to-action buttons such as “Book now!” and “Learn more.”

Branding Project with Figma

We began with individual moodboards and brand guides, exploring simplistic and uninspiring design prototypes. After receiving feedback, I proposed a transformative new design that received unanimous team approval and perfectly aligned with the client’s brand identity. This design was even used by the client for her business cards.

Portfolio Development

I experimented with different styles inspired by the ocean and simplicity before finalizing a design based on my country's history. I incorporated symbols from native Indian and Arawak tribe paintings found in Aruba’s caves and created a vision board to guide the design process. After finalizing the aesthetic, I began designing, ultimately adding some color to enhance the design.

Redesigning a stagnant company website resulted in a layout that aligned with industry standards and provided a more user-friendly experience. The branding project with Figma allowed me to develop an interactive website mockup that perfectly matched the client’s brand identity, receiving positive feedback from stakeholders. My portfolio development involved an iterative design process and incorporating feedback, which helped refine the layout and ensured the final product was polished and met both project requirements and client expectations.
